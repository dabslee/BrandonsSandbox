Evidence Card C

Evidence

As shown on the evidence card, the Rivera Plate is moving towards the North American Plate at around 3 centimeters per year.

Reasoning

The definition of a convergent boundary is "a major geologic discontinuity or suture marking the juncture of lithospheric plates that have been joined by plate tectonics." In other words, a convergent boundary is where two plates go towards each other, which is clearly shown here.
